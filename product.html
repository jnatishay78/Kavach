Oximeter

How to use:

  1. Remove any nail polish/false nails & warm your hand if cold.
  2. Rest for at least 5 minutes before taking your measurement.
  3. Rest your hand on your chest at heart level & hold it still.
  4. Switch on the oximeter & place it on your middle or index finger.
  5. The reading takes time to steady, Keep the oximeter in place for at least a minute or longer if the reading is not stable.
  6. Record the highest result once it has not changed for 5 seconds.
  7. Identify each reading carefully.
  8. Start recording from baseline & record three times a day at the same time. Take extra measures if you feel a change in your health.

Benefits

  1. Monitoring oxygen saturation over time
  2. Alerting to dangerously low oxygen levels, particularly in new-borns
  3. Offering peace of mind to people with chronic respiratory or cardiovascular conditions
  4. Assessing the need for supplemental oxygen
  5. Monitoring oxygen saturation levels in people under anaesthesia
  6. Indicating dangerous side effects in people taking drugs that affect breathing or oxygen saturation
